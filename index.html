Time line of Betty Bacall's life:

  • 1924 Born as Betty Joan Perske in New York City to Jewish immigrants William Perske and Natalie Weinstein-Bacal.
  • 1930 Her parents divorce. Bacall later refers to her father as "that bastard" and says: "He wasn't a wonderful man"
  • 1942 She makes her Broadway debut in Johnny Two by Four using the stage name Betty Bacall. The name Bacall is adopted from her mother's maiden name, Bacal.
  • 1943 Receives work as a regular fashion model for Harper's Bazaar.
  • 1943 After Bacall is featured on the cover of Harper's Bazaar, the wife of director Howard Haws notices her and tells Howard about her
  • 1945 Stars in To Have and Have Not with Humphrey Bogart. Her performance in this film earns her the nickname, "The Look," because of her signature pose in which she turns her head down and looks up in a knowing way.
  • 1945 Bacall makes headlines when she visits the National Press Club in Washington D.C. and, as a publicity stunt, sits on Vice President Harry S. Truman's piano while he plays. The photos caused a scandal. Truman's wife Bess was upset.
  • 1945 Marries Bogart at Malabar Farm in Mansfield, Ohio.
  • 1946 Stars in The Big Sleep with Bogart
  • 1948 Stars in Key Largo with Bogart and Lionel Barrymore
  • 1949 Gives birth to son Stephen Humphrey Bogart. He is named for Bogart's character Steve in To Have and Have Not.
